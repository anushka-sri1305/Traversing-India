About Rishikesh

Rishikesh, a serene town in Uttarakhand, India, is globally recognized as the Yoga Capital of the World. Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas and along the sacred Ganges River, it is a significant pilgrimage site and a hub for spiritual seekers, nature enthusiasts, and adventurers alike. Rishikesh has a deep connection to Hindu mythology, with legends of Lord Rama and Lakshmana tied to its sacred sites. The Lakshman Jhula and Ram Jhula suspension bridges are iconic landmarks. Spiritual centers such as Parmarth Niketan Ashram and the evening Ganga Aarti at Triveni Ghat attract visitors seeking peace and enlightenment.

This tranquil town rose to global fame in the 1960s when The Beatles visited the Maharishi Mahesh Yogi’s ashram. Today, it hosts the International Yoga Festival, where practitioners from around the world immerse themselves in ancient practices. Yoga schools and meditation retreats offer courses that draw seekers year-round.

Rishikesh is equally famous for its thrilling white-water rafting on the Ganges, bungee jumping, and trekking in nearby Himalayan trails. The pristine Neer Garh Waterfall and the tranquil Vashishta Cave are favorite spots for nature lovers. Rishikesh is a harmonious blend of spirituality, culture, and adventure. Its natural beauty, historical significance, and peaceful ambiance make it a must-visit destination for anyone seeking rejuvenation and inspiration. Whether you're a devotee, a yoga enthusiast, or an adventurer, Rishikesh offers an unforgettable experience.

Places to Visit

Lakshman Jhula

Lakshman Jhula is an iconic 450-foot-long suspension bridge in Rishikesh, spanning the Ganga River. It is steeped in mythology, believed to be the spot where Lord Lakshman crossed the river on a jute rope. The bridge offers stunning views and connects religious sites, markets, and ashrams

Triveni Ghat

Triveni Ghat is a sacred riverfront in Rishikesh where the Ganga, Yamuna, and Saraswati rivers are believed to converge. Famous for its evening Ganga Aarti, it is a serene spot for prayers and rituals, attracting pilgrims and tourists seeking spiritual solace

Beatles Ashram

The Beatles Ashram, officially known as the Chaurasi Kutia, is a historic site in Rishikesh where the Beatles stayed in 1968 to study Transcendental Meditation under Maharishi Mahesh Yogi. Now an eco-tourism spot, it features graffiti art, meditation huts, and tranquil surroundings, attracting music lovers and spiritual seekers​

How to Reach Rishikesh from New Delhi

Traveling from New Delhi to Rishikesh offers several options, each catering to different preferences and budgets:


By Train

Rishikesh has a small railway station, but Haridwar, located 25 km away, is the major hub with excellent connectivity to Delhi. Trains from Delhi to Haridwar take around 4-6 hours. From Haridwar, you can reach Rishikesh via taxi or a short bus ride.

By Road

The road journey from New Delhi to Rishikesh spans approximately 230 km and takes around 5–7 hours depending on traffic and the route. The preferred route is via NH 334, which passes through Meerut, Muzaffarnagar, and Haridwar. You can drive your own car, hire a taxi, or take state or private buses. Private taxis provide comfort and flexibility for stops, while buses are economical.

By Air

Rishikesh does not have an airport, but the nearest one is Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun, about 35 km away. From Delhi, you can fly to Dehradun (a 1-hour flight) and then take a taxi or bus to Rishikesh, which takes another 1–1.5 hours. Flights are convenient for those who want to minimize travel time

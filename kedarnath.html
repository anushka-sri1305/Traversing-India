About Kedarnath

Kedarnath, nestled in the majestic Himalayan range in the Indian state of Uttarakhand, is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites in India. It is part of the Char Dham Yatra, a spiritual journey that includes Yamunotri, Gangotri, and Badrinath. Kedarnath is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is believed to be the place where the Pandavas sought forgiveness for their sins. The Kedarnath Temple, built by the Pandavas, is a marvel of ancient stone architecture and a place of immense spiritual significance.

The temple is located at an altitude of 3,583 meters (11,755 feet) above sea level, surrounded by towering snow-capped peaks and glaciers, creating a serene and awe-inspiring atmosphere. The trek to Kedarnath is challenging, but the breathtaking views and divine blessings make the journey worthwhile. Thousands of pilgrims visit Kedarnath every year, braving the difficult terrain and weather conditions to experience the holiness and beauty of this sacred place. Apart from its religious significance, Kedarnath is also a place of tranquility, making it a favorite destination for spiritual seekers and nature enthusiasts alike.

The temple is open for a limited period during the summer months due to heavy snowfall during winters. Visitors are encouraged to plan their trips between May and October, which is considered the ideal time to visit Kedarnath. The spiritual journey to this holy place offers a perfect blend of devotion, adventure, and scenic beauty, making it an unforgettable experience for all who visit.

Places to Visit

Sonprayag

Sonprayag is a small town located at the confluence of the rivers Mandakini and Son Ganga. It serves as the base for the trek to Kedarnath and is the point from where you can take a jeep to Gaurikund.

Gaurikund

Gaurikund is the starting point for the trek to Kedarnath. It is a sacred place dedicated to Goddess Parvati and is known for its hot water spring. From here, the trek begins, leading pilgrims towards the Kedarnath temple.

Guptkashi

Located en route to Kedarnath, Guptkashi is a beautiful town known for its ancient temples. It is often visited by pilgrims before proceeding to Kedarnath, and it offers picturesque views of the surrounding mountains.

How to Reach Kedarnath from New Delhi

Reaching Kedarnath from New Delhi requires a combination of train, road, and trekking. The journey takes you through picturesque towns, valleys, and rivers, offering you a chance to experience the serene beauty of the Himalayan region.


By Train

The nearest railway stations to Kedarnath are Haridwar and Rishikesh. From New Delhi, you can board a train to either of these stations. The train journey from New Delhi to Haridwar takes around 5 to 6 hours, while the journey to Rishikesh takes about 6 hours. Once you reach Haridwar or Rishikesh, you can take a bus or hire a taxi to reach Sonprayag, which is the starting point for the trek to Kedarnath. The road journey from Haridwar or Rishikesh to Sonprayag takes approximately 8 to 10 hours, depending on traffic and weather conditions.

By Road

You can travel by road from Haridwar or Rishikesh to Sonprayag, which is about 210 km from Haridwar. Sonprayag is well-connected by roads from major towns in Uttarakhand. From Sonprayag, take a shared jeep to Gaurikund (approximately 5-6 km), which is the base for the Kedarnath trek. The road from Haridwar to Sonprayag passes through the scenic towns of Rishikesh, Devprayag, and Rudraprayag, offering travelers a chance to enjoy the stunning landscapes of the region.

Trekking

Once you reach Gaurikund, the trek to Kedarnath begins. The trek is about 16 kilometers long and takes around 6-8 hours to complete, depending on your pace and physical fitness. The trail is steep in some areas but offers spectacular views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The journey to Kedarnath on foot is a spiritual experience, with the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas offering a divine backdrop throughout the trek.
